NAVIS Hantavirus Participation Guidance

NAVIS is an observational study investigating the natural history of Andes virus infection and exposure. Participating sites contribute clinical, laboratory, and follow-up data using ISARIC-supported study tools and infrastructure. The steps below describe how new sites can join the study and begin data collection.

Step 1 — Review the Study Documents

Before joining the study, please review the core study documentation to understand the study design, procedures, and requirements.

Links
  • Study Protocol
  • Participant Information Sheets / Consent Forms (if applicable)
Action: Review the core study documents before proceeding.

Step 2 — Obtain Local Approvals

Participating sites are responsible for obtaining all required local approvals before study activities begin.

This may include
  • Ethics / IRB approval
  • Institutional approvals
  • Biosafety approvals
  • Regulatory approvals (where applicable)
Links
  • Template protocol package
  • Ethics support documents
  • Model consent forms
Action: Secure all required local approvals before site initiation.

Step 3 — Complete Site Participation Documents

Before receiving access to the NAVIS study platform, participating sites must complete the required collaboration and data-sharing documentation.

This includes
  • Signing the ISARIC Terms of Submission
  • Completing the Site CRF survey with:
    • Institution information
    • Site details
    • List of study personnel participating in the project
Links
  • ISARIC Terms of Submission
  • Site CRF survey
  • Data governance documentation
Action: Submit the signed Terms of Submission and completed Site CRF survey.

Step 4 — Complete Training

Participating sites are responsible for ensuring that all study personnel receive appropriate training before study activities and data entry begin.

This includes familiarisation with
  • Study procedures
  • REDCap use
  • CRF completion guidance
  • Local data protection and information governance requirements

Training and guidance materials are provided to support site onboarding; however, each site is responsible for ensuring that relevant staff are adequately trained and that local training records are maintained where required.

Links
  • CRF Completion Guide
  • ISARIC Data Entry Manual
  • REDCap Training Materials (External)
Action: Ensure all relevant staff are trained before data collection begins.

Step 5 — Request REDCap Access

Once approvals and training are complete, users can request database access. To request REDCap access, please email data@isaric.org from your institutional email address with the full name(s) of the person or people requiring access and their institutional affiliation.

The email should copy the Site Lead / Principal Investigator, who must reply to confirm approval before access can be granted. Individual user accounts are required for all users; shared accounts are not permitted.

Action: Email data@isaric.org with the access request and Site Lead copied.

Step 6 — Review the CRFs and Data Collection Tools

Before enrolment begins, sites should familiarise themselves with the study forms and workflows.

Documents
  • CRF
  • Data Dictionary
Action: Review the CRFs and data dictionary before starting enrolment.

Step 7 — Local Hosting

Participating sites may choose to host their own local REDCap database for the study.

To maximise compatibility and facilitate harmonised data collection across sites, it is strongly recommended that sites use the same database structure and version as the central NAVIS database.
